about

i'm a computer science major with a user experience design minor and a web development certificate at western washington university. i'm the president of the agic (association for gender inclusion in computing), i design and create websites like this one, take pictures (mostly portraits) on my pentax k1000 film camera, propogate as many plants as i can get my hands on, knit whatever i can to keep my friends cozy, and a bunch more. scroll down to learn more about me and my creative endeavors!

visual communication (gallery )

during my years at wwu, my favorite classes have always been the classes i've taken for my ux minor. my sophomore year, i took the hardest class i've taken to date: the foundations of visual communications. it was a three hour long class twice a week, and i had to fill eight 8.5" by 11" pages per week. it made my 400 level systems classes look easy. before this class, i thought i was terrible at drawing. but that quarter i learned that the biggest obstacle wasn't my skill, but my own perfectionism. foundations of visual comm taught me how to be bad at things on the first try, how to be confident and decisive in my motions- to draw without using a pencil before a pen, and though my own exporations in recreating the objects and space around me, i learned an entirely different way to see the world - the parts and how they create their sums.

web development

after taking an introductory web development class in my freshman year, i found a love for designing and creating websites. i discovered that it fed my creative and analytical sides equally, which puts me in my flow state (please, ask me about flow psychology). i have two websites that are published aside from my own; the first is myfiverules.com , which was a job i took customizing a squarespace site. the second site i built from the ground up for one of my favorite creative friends - kentakesphotos.com .

photography

in my senior year of high school, i took a film photography class to fill my art credits. at first, i didn't get the appeal, because i started the class taking pictures of "things" (our first assignment was "person, place, thing"). as soon as i developed my roll for "person", i realized that black and white film can show you what a person really looks like. it shows all of the things you can see when you're around someone in real life - their energy, how they feel, how they make others feel, how they present themselves in the world. the question i love the most always comes after a smile when i show someone their film portraits: "is that what i really look like?"
